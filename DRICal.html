DRI


DRI stands for dietary reference intake. The DRIs tell us how much water, calories, macronutrients, and vitamins a given person should consume daily - the entire estimation is based on sex, age, and activity level.

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate)

Amount of energy (calories) your body needs to maintain basic physiological functions like breathing, circulating blood, and regulating body temperature while at rest. It's the minimum energy requirement for your body to function.

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

Total number of calories you need to maintain your current weight, considering all your daily activities, including exercise, walking, and even digestion. TDEE accounts for your BMR plus any additional calories burned through daily activities and physical activity.

Caloric Needs for Weight Loss (20% deficit)

To lose weight, you need to consume fewer calories than you burn. A 20% caloric deficit means you're consuming 20% fewer calories than your TDEE, which should lead to gradual weight loss. In this case, you'd aim to consume around 2025.02 calories per day to lose weight.

Caloric Needs for Weight Gain (20% surplus)

To gain weight, you need to consume more calories than you burn. A 20% caloric surplus means you're consuming 20% more calories than your TDEE, promoting weight gain. In this scenario, you'd aim to consume around 3037.54 calories per day to gain weight.
